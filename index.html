Waqf

Def.: A permanent dedication of the corpus of the valuable property by a Muslim to the ownership of Allah for religious, charitable, and pious purposes.

  • Waqf property cannot be sold, mortgaged, donated, or alienated, even by inheritance.

  • Only the income produced by Waqf property can be consumed. The Waqf property itself cannot be consumed.

  • Charity to family members is better than charity to strangers. Please see ḥadīth of Abū Hurayrah.

Pillars of Waqf

  1. Founder واقف

  2. Waqf property موقوف

  3. Beneficiaries موقوف عليه

  4. Expression صيغة

The conditions of the واقف must be respected unless they are invalid Sharīʿah wise.

Al-Waṣiyyah

Def: A gift that is given after the death of the موصي.

Waṣiyyah existed in the nations before the Prophet Muhammad (Peace be upon him). See hadith of ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAmr.

Kafārah exists even for actions that are considered muʿāmalāt. Example: al-Ẓihār.

  1. It is a gift from Allah the Mighty and Exalted.

  2. It cannot be given to an heir who will inherit. The distinction is important in English as there can be heirs who will not inherit anything.

  3. The limit of waṣiyyah is \$1/3\$ of the مال. Evidence for this is the ḥadīth of Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqās

Waṣiyyah that is greater than one-third

If directed to a stranger, will be given if all the heirs AGREE, otherwise, it will be reduced to the maximum, i.e. one-third, and then given.

If directed to an heir (who will inherit), it will be given if all the other heirs AGREE, otherwise it will be invalidated.

Wardship

It is not recommended to become a guardian of orphans' wealth. See the ḥadīth of Abū Dharr.

The benefit of the child comes first. Preservation of religion is the greatest benefit.

A person cannot buy from his ward since there is a clash of interests.

According to (some) Ḥanafīs, Zakāt must be paid on all wealth irrespective of who it belongs to. Be they a ward, an insane person, etc…​

Deprivers

Assuming at least one of the أسباب is present, only the following may deprive one of inheritance.

Inheritance Deprivers

  1. Kufr

  2. Murder

  3. Slavery

The murder that is intended when discussing the deprivation of inheritance is the murder of the deceased by one or more of his inheriting heirs.

Murder

Types of Murder

  1. العَمْدُ Intentional

  2. شِبْهُ العَمْدِ Looks intentional (Ex: A child dies due to beating by a stick.)

  3. الخَطَأُ Unintentional

According to the Shāfiʿīs, murder of any of these kinds deprives a person from inheritance irrespective of his position to the murder (witness, judge, witness of behavior to a witness, etc…​) except for TWO people:

  1. A ḥadīth narrator (al-Rāwī)

  2. A Muftī who passes judgement of death (This requires clarification)

Slavery

Types of Slaves

  1. القِنُّ

  2. المُبَعَضُ

    1. A partial slave. Example: Two people buy a slave and then one of them manumits his respective part.

    2. According to the Ḥanbalīs, such a slave may inherit through his free part.

  3. المُكَاتَب

    1. Needs to pay a certain amount to his master in order to manumit himself.

    2. Involves some issues with zakāt.

  4. المُدَبَّرُ

    1. Becomes free after the death of the master due to an agreement by the latter.

  5. أم ولد

    1. Consists of two issues.

      1. Can she be sold?

      2. Does she become free when you die? Yes. The slave is part of the estate of the master and as such will be inherited. Since children cannot enslave their parents and vice versa, the mother will be manumitted. This answer assumes that at least one child will inherit her.

Marrying a slave-girl

Marrying a slave-girl is not allowed except in TWO cases:

  1. If he cannot afford to marry a free woman.

  2. He fears with certainty that he will commit zinā if he doesn’t marry the slave-girl.

Heirs

Types of Inheriting Heirs

  1. Males (15 of them)

  2. Females (10 of them)

Males

  1. Son الإبن

  2. Son’s Son ابن الإبن (وإن نزل)

  3. Father الأب

  4. Father’s Father أب الأب (وأن علا بمحض الذكور)

  5. Full Brother الأخ من الأبوين

  6. Consanguine Brother الأخ من الأب

  7. Uterine Brother الأخ من الأم

  8. Full Brother’s Son ابن الأخ من الأبوين (وإن نزل)

  9. Consanguine Brother’s Son ابن الأخ من الأب (وإن نزل)

  10. Father’s Full Brother العم من الأبوين (وإن علا)

  11. Fathers' Consanguine Brother العم من الأب (وإن علا)

  12. Father’s Full Brother’s Son ابن العم من الأبوين (وإن نزل)

  13. Father’s Consanguine Brother’s Son ابن العم من الأب (وإن نزل)

  14. Husband الزوج

  15. المعتِق

Females

  1. Daughter البنت

  2. Son’s Daughter بنت الأبن (وإن نزل)

  3. Mother الأم

  4. Mother’s Mother أم الأم (وإن علت بمحض الإناث)

  5. Father’s Mother أم الأب (وإن علت بمحض الإناث) أو أم الأب (وإن علا بمحض الذكور)

  6. Full Sister الأخت من الأبوين

  7. Consanguine Sister الأخت من الأب

  8. Uterine Sister الأخت من الأم

  9. Wife الزوجة

  10. المعتقة

Any (grand)mother that is connected to someone who inherits, will inherit.
Categories

  1. الأصول - F, FF, M, MM, FM

  2. الفروع - S, D, SS, SD

  3. هواشي - Everyone else

Types of portions for inheritors

  1. Taʿṣīb — without fixed portion

    1. All the males (15) are included here except the husband (-1), uterine brother (-1). Al-Muʿtiqah (+1) is also here. Total of 14.

  2. Farḍ — with fixed portion

    1. All the females (10) are included here except al-Muʿtiqah (-1). The husband (+1) and uterine brother(+1) are also included. Total of 11.

Al-Furūḍ al-Muqaddarah

There are 6. They are \$\frac{1}{2}\$, \$\frac{1}{4}\$, \$\frac{1}{6}\$, \$\frac{1}{8}\$, \$\frac{1}{3}\$ and \$\frac{2}{3}\$.

Uṣūl al-Masāʾil

There are two divisions:

  1. SEVEN Agreed upon bases: \$2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24\$.

  2. TWO Disagreed upon bases: \$18, 36\$.

Al-Furūḍ’s Distribution

1/2

  1. Daughter

    1. On condition that she’s the only child.

  2. Son’s Daughter

    1. On condition that she’s the only child and only grandchild.

  3. Full Sister

    1. No male ʿAṣl is alive.

    2. No Farʿ is alive.

    3. No other full sibling.

  4. Consanguine Sister

    1. No male ʿAṣl is alive.

    2. No Farʿ is alive.

    3. No full siblings.

    4. No other consanguine sibling.

  5. Husband

    1. The deceased has no farʿ.

    2. Cannot be blocked.

1/4

  1. Husband

    1. The deceased has farʿ.

    2. Cannot be blocked.

  2. Wife

    1. The deceased has no farʿ.

    2. Cannot be blocked.

1/8

  1. Wife

    1. The deceased has farʿ.

    2. Cannot be blocked.

2/3

  1. Everyone under 1/2 except the husband.

    1. No other male siblings.

    2. Other female (full?/consanguine?) siblings should be present

    3. Other conditions from 1/2 apply.

1/3

  1. Mother

    1. No child or grandchild (وإن نزل).

    2. There shouldn’t be a group of more than one sibling (any kind: consanguine, full, or uterine).

    3. The inheritance case should not be from the (الغرويين).

  2. Uterine siblings (weakest entities in inheritance matters)

    1. No male ʿAṣl is alive.

    2. No child or grandchild (وإن نزل).

    3. They have to be more than one.

    4. The principle of one male to two females ratio doesn’t apply here.

1/6

  1. Father

    1. Only when there is a child or grandchild (وإن نزل).

  2. Mother

    1. When there is فرع الوارث.

    2. If there is a group of more than one sibling.

  3. Father’s Father

    1. Father is not alive.

    2. No فرع الوارث exist.

    3. The inheritance case should not be the case of الجد والإخوة.

  4. Any Grandmother

    1. Mother is not alive.

  5. Son’s Daughter

    1. No male child or male grandchild (وإن نزل).

    2. The deceased is survived by only one daughter.

  6. Consanguine Sister

    1. No male ʿAṣl.

    2. No فرع الوارث exist.

    3. No (consanguine or full) male sibling.

    4. Only one full sister exists.

  7. Uterine Sibling

    1. No male ʿAṣl.

    2. No فرع الوارث exist.

    3. He/She has to be the only sibling.

Al-Taʿṣīb (ʿAṣabah)

Literal meaning: the tightening.

Types of ʿAṣabah

  1. ʿAṣabah bi al-Nafs عصبة بالنفس

    1. Every male except husband and uterine brother.

  2. ʿAṣabah bi al-Ghayr عصبة بالغير

    1. They are ʿaṣīb due to another person and they inherit with them (the same portion is assigned to both).

    2. The groups are {S, D}, {SS, SD}, {FB, FS}, and {CB, CS}.

    3. When a group exists, they share the inheritance [and the ratio of 1:2 doesn’t apply.] (CLARIFY)

  3. ʿAṣabah maʿ al-Ghayr عصبة مع غير

    1. They are ʿaṣīb due to another person and they inherit due to them (different portions are assigned to them).

    2. They are upgraded to the rank of their male counterpart.

    3. .Groups are {FS with D or SD}

Ḥadīth References

Deeds coming to an end

The Messenger (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) said: “When a man dies, his good deeds come to an end except three: ongoing charity, beneficial knowledge and a righteous child who will pray for him.” — Muslim, no. 3084.

Waqf Basis

It is narrated that ʿUmar (may Allah be pleased with him) said: “O Messenger of Allah, I have got wealth from Khaybar and I have nothing that is more precious to me than that. What do you command me to do with it?” He said, “If you wish, you can put it aside and give in charity from it (from what it produces), but the original property should not be sold, given away or inherited.” So ʿUmar gave it in charity to the poor and to relatives, used it to set slaves free, gave it for the sake of Allaah, helped wayfarers and honoured his guests. — Al-Bukhārī and Muslim.

Charity to Family

Abū Hurayrah (May Allah be pleased with him) narrated that the Prophet (Peace be upon him) commanded to give sadaqah. A man said: O Messenger of Allah, I have a dīnār. He (Peace be upon him) said: Spend it on yourself. He again said: I have another. He (Peace be upon him) said: Spend it on your children. He again said: I have another. He (Peace be upon him) said: Spend it on your wife. He again said: I have another. He (Peace be upon him) said: Spend it on your servant. He finally said: I have another. He (Peace be upon him) replied: You know best (what to do with it). — Abū Dāwūd, no. 1691.

Waṣiyyah before Prophet Muḥammad

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAmr said, “We were sitting with the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, when a bedouin man wearing a robe with a border approached him until he stood before the Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace. He said, ‘Your companion has debased every horseman and elevates every shepherd.’ The Prophet, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, took hold of the folds of his robe and said, ‘I see that you are wearing the clothes of someone who is without intelligence.’ Then he went on, ‘When the Prophet Nūḥ, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, told his son, “I will give you some instructions. I command you two things and I forbid you two things. I command you to say, ‘There is no god but Allah.’ If the seven heavens and the seven earths were a dark ring, they would be cut by ‘There is no god but Allah’ and ‘Glory be to Allah and by His praise.’ It is the prayer of every thing and by it everything has its provision. And I forbid you to associate others with and Allah and to be proud.”’” — Imam al-Bukhārī in al-Adab al-Mufrad (no. 548)

Will Limit

It was narrated that Saʿd ibn Abī Waqqāṣ said: “The Messenger of Allah visited me when I was sick, and said: ‘Have you made a will?’ I said: ‘Yes.’ He said: ‘How much?’ I said: ‘For all my wealth to be given in the cause of Allah.’ He said: ‘What have you left for your children?’ I said: ‘They are rich (independent of means).’ He said: ‘Bequeath one-tenth.’ And we kept discussing it until he said: ‘Bequeath one-third, and one-third is much or large.’” — Al-Nasāʾī, no. 3631.

Governance of Orphans' Wealth

Abū Dharr narrated that the Messenger of of Allah (Peace be upon him) said, “Abū Dharr, I find that you are weak and I like for you what I like for myself. Do not rule over (even) two persons and do not manage the property of an orphan.” — Muslim, no. 1826.
